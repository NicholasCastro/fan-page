Like any good movie, one of Big Hero 6's strenghts is its cast of round characters. The protagonist, antagonist, and supporting cast all have their own personalities and problems.

  • Hiro Hatashi: The movie's protagonist is a smart but troubled child who's grief over his brother's death pushes the plot forward. While he is incredibly inteligent, his grief occasionaly causes him to make brash, impulsive decisions.
  • Baymax: The movie's main supporting character is a robot that is portrayed so brilliantly you forget he isn't human. He mainly serves as a reminder of Hiro's brother's life.
  • Hiro's Friends: The other four supporting characters, Fred, GoGo Tomago, Wasabi, and Honey Lemon keep the movie fresh by showcasing a variety of personalities, from the comic book geek to the enthusiastic chick.
  • Robert Callaghan: The antagonist (SPOILER!) of the film whose loss mirrors that of Hiro's. Unlike Hatashi, he deals with his grief by seeking vengeance.

Disney has never been afraid to incorporate more mature themes into their PG movies, and like The Lion King before it, Big Hero 6 deals with the difficulty of overcoming loss. However, unlike The Lion King, this movie has both the hero and the villian face the same problem. Hiro has his friends, especially Baymax, to help him get through the loss of his brother. Callaghan, on the other hand, has no one to comfort him and, therefore, resorted to violence and anger as a response to the loss of his daughter.
